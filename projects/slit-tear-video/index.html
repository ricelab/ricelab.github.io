Slit-Tear Video Exploration

A novel metaphor for exploring and understanding video

We explore the use of interactive slit-scanning for the purpose of video exploration. We show how such interactive slits (which we call slit-tears) allow for trivial identification of “interesting” events in video (Tang, Greenberg, & Fels, 2008; Tang, Greenberg, & Fels, 2009). This timeline is interactive, and allows for recovery of the original video frame. We argue that the slit-tear technique can be used to simplify computer vision techniques (Tang, Lanir, Greenberg, & Fels, 2008), as the resulting visualizations are simple 2D representations of the original scene. That, and it’s pretty wicked cool to look and play with.
